Burden of Proof (BOP)


This is a fun one, and also one of the most important sales principles.


Imagine you want to convince your friend of something.


And then he tells you:


“Prove it.”


And you go, “Well it’s because this, because that, because blah blah blah”


What did he (unknowingly) just do to you in that interaction?


He just gave you the BOP.




BOP stands for:


B: Burden
O: Of
P: Proof




This is when you give someone the burden of proving themselves to you…


Whoever has to prove themselves to the other person in an interaction, and speak more, is always in the weaker position (see Principle of Investment).


Whatever words you make THEM say, are 10x more powerful than anything YOU could have said to THEM (see Make Them Sell to Themselves).


BOP also gives the person an incentive to reply by shifting the conversation back to them and getting them to talk.




There are 2 principles to using the BOP:


1. Get Them to Speak in the First Place.
2. Make Them Say What YOU Want.




1 - Get Them to Speak in the First Place


You can get them to speak about themselves through questions or commands.


Place them at the end of a spoken sentence, or as a standalone phrase.


Keep in mind that phrasing something as a command can sometimes be deemed insulting. Make sure you understand the frame (see Frame) you have with the person you are speaking with, so that you know when it is appropriate to use a question, or a command.




2 - Make Them Say What YOU Want


Guide them to say the words you want them to say via specific questions asked and commands given.


Questions:


- What do you think about this?
- Can you prove this?
- Why are you doing this?
- Am I someone special to you?
- When’s the last time you did this?
- How do you think this works?
- Do you understand what goes into this?


Commands:


- Prove to me that is true.
- Explain to me how it works.
- Tell me why you are doing this.
- Explain.
- Elaborate.




Emotional sell


Every sale is an emotional sell.


The exact moment that you decide to pull out your credit card and purchase something… is an EMOTIONAL decision.


You may have heard “prospects justify their emotions with reason”.


But that is only partly accurate, and let me explain why…




Logic Influences Emotions


You need logic before the sale is made, because that logic will influence people to feel SAFE in their decisions.


Read that again, the more logical reassurance someone can get about purchasing a product or service, the SAFER they will feel about it.


Further…


AFTER the sale is made, the logic utilized in justifying the purchase, will give the prospect a feeling of relief, gratitude, and certainty in their decision.


So at every step of the buying process, emotions are once again king.




Know How To Use Emotion


Taking a directly emotional approach and ignoring the logic required to purchase doesn’t work.


And neither does focusing all on logic, and totally ignoring a direct approach.


You must strike a balance depending on the prospect.


Using logic is quite straightforward. Here you posit arguments, analogies, describe benefits, attach them to problems, and show proof.


Using emotion is a bit more complicated, you’ve got to understand the prospect first.


During the FACT FINDING phase, you should be able to dig into their desires and emotions surrounding their interest in your offer (the degree to which is dependent on the niche, some niches are inherently more emotional than others).


Once you know these desires and emotions, it’s time to utilize them:


* First choose the desire (because a desire is attached to an emotion, you don’t want to overtly say “so that you will feel proud of achieving X”) you want to use.
* Use this desire and weave it into the presentation, by hinting that it is a desire satisfied by the features of the offer.
* During objection handling, you will handle mostly logical objections, but once you get to the emotional barriers, it’s time to pull out these desire triggers once again.
* Use any variety/combination of appropriate sales techniques (for example 3 yes rule, can be others), and remind them of their desires by painting a picture of what happens if they don’t address their desires.
* Use any variety/combination of appropriate sales techniques, and bring them out of the nightmare scenario and paint the dream scenario where their desires are totally fulfilled.




What Is Happening?


Their desires trigger emotions.


This is how you build the desire curve, which is increasing their desire in an offer, which in turn increases their emotions.


When it comes time to buy, they should be in a heightened emotional state in order to buy.


This will not always come 100% as a direct result of your efforts, marketing will do a lot of work too (and same principles of triggering people’s desires apply to marketing as well).


You may not close them THIS time, but maybe the next time, or the time after that.


Their desire may creep up based on circumstantial life events at an unexpected moment, and then you will land an easy sale.


Humans are usually simple creatures, but the processes which guide when they will be ready emotionally to buy can be erratic and complicated.


Now you are equipped with the tools to understand, and utilize the periphery of these processes (because you cannot fully understand and control a random affect of a human process) to your advantage.




Make people sell to themselves


People will sell to themselves if you ask them to.


Whatever anyone can say to themselves, is infinitely more convincing than whatever you can tell them.




How To Do This?


- Ask them to describe their problems to you (selling themselves on their problems).
- Ask them to describe their ideal scenario to you (selling themselves on their dreams).
- Ask them to describe how they would imagine your product/service helping them (selling themselves on how you have the best solution for them).




If You Are Smart, You Will Notice…


That a lot of these questions you can ask them relate directly to BOP questions.


Astute observation.


The burden of proof is on them to sell THEMSELVES on whatever idea you want them to think.


Because you can formulate question to engineer replies, such as when you ask someone:


“How many people are inside the program?”


“X”


“And how many people have achieved success?”


“X”


“So that is 100%, correct?”


“Yes”


“So what makes you think that you cannot also do the same?”


See how they come to the conclusion themselves?




3 Yes Rule


The 3 yes rule is when you ask a series of questions engineered to elicit a “yes” response.


The theory is that after saying yes 3 times, saying yes a 4th time is X% more likely to happen.


Keep in mind that for this to work properly, you must have the first question be the easiest to answer “yes” to. Followed by the 2nd and 3rd questions in terms of ease of answering. This is because even though the 4th and final questions have to be the hardest hitting… Each question before it should get you in the habit of saying “yes”, so it already affects the outcome of the next question, which is why you can make saying “yes” a little bit more difficult each time.


Also because it is important to use a logical flow of reason that builds off each previous point to maintain congruence and heighten believability.




For Example…


When trying to convince people to eat raw meat:


1. Do you believe that the governments are not always telling the truth about health?
2. Do you believe that given they are not always telling you the truth, that there are perhaps suppressed methods of cellular regeneration that cannot be sold and those financial incentives are why they are not telling you the truth?
3. Do you believe that if every person on earth had access to such knowledge then much of the pharmaceutical and medical industry would no longer be able to profit as they would be obsolete and outclassed in this field? 
4. So do you consider the possibility they tell you that raw meat is bad for this very reason?


Now this tool alone won’t convince someone to eat raw meat. But do you see how it is already much more powerful than simply telling someone off the bat that raw meat will regenerate their cells?




The 3 Yes Rule Can Also Be Used in Reverse to Elicit A “No” Response.


For example, proving someone’s innocence:


1. Would someone with unlimited financial resources have any motivation to commit a petty scam for a small sum of money?
2. Would such a person ruin his good name built up over 10+ years for a little bit of money?
3. Assuming that at some point you will always be attacked for something when you become successful, does it make any sense to 100% trust the accuser’s claims off the bat?
4. So then do you think it’s fair how the defendant is being unjustly portrayed in the media given all of these things?




The 3 Yes Rule Can Also Become The “2 Yes Rule” To Weaker Effect…


Or the “4 Yes Rule”, with diminishing returns.


Something about the number 3 is the sweet spot.




Ben Franklin Effect


The Ben Franklin effect is when you become more likable and/or trustworthy to a person because you asked them for a favor.


Seems counterintuitive, you would think asking someone for a favor somewhat damages your relationship with them.


And this is true, ONLY when the favor request is not accepted.


When the favor request IS accepted, then that favor becomes an investment on the end of the person you are asking.


They are invested in you, and as such will view you as more likable and/or trustworthy.


This can sometimes also be labeled as “Compliance”, and we will get into how you can use it to your advantage.




But First Some Examples…


* In dating, when you ask a woman for a small favor for example, you are building investment on her end. Eventually this investment compounds and she is giving you even greater levels of time, energy, and attention.
* In finance, the more you buy a stock, the more bias you have towards that stock. If it goes down, you are likely to hold and not sell in the hopes that it goes back up again. Similarly if it goes up, you will buy more and chase the price for even greater returns. This can also be called sunk cost fallacy.
* Another dating example, who is the woman you remember the most? The woman who brought you nothing but peace in your relationship? Or the woman who brought chaos and drama that you had to deal with on your end? Answer honestly.
* And finally a business example, the project you’ve spent the most time and effort on is usually the one that is harder to quit and cut losses on right? No matter what the empirical evidence is saying, part of you will want to keep it running because of how much investment you’ve already put into it.


This principle of the Ben Franklin Effect can be labeled in many different ways in many different contexts, but the mechanism is the same.




So How Do You Utilize It To Your Advantage in Sales and Marketing?


The possibilities are limited only to your creativity, here are some of my favorite uses:


* Ask a lead to send you a short paragraph as to why they want to join your program so that you can show your superiors so that it can help convince them to give the lead superior consideration.
* Getting a lead to talk about themselves early on by asking questions, ie. “Tell me about yourself”.
* Asking your target audience a small favor such as to perform a task, ie. “Look at this” or “Think about this”. You can stack these multiple times subtly in your marketing semantics for compounding effect.
* Asking a lead to hold off on sending you money, while you complete some sort of task for them. This one’s a savage tactic. Use this only when they are almost sold, and just need some encouragement to cross the finish line. What this does is that it amplifies the anxiety they have to get started with your product/service, so that when you DO ask them to finally send the money, they do so even more enthusiastically. The time waiting they are investing into the idea of finally sending money for the product/service they want. This is usually a great way to get a well sold, but hesitant lead over the line, because you also build great trust by denying their money for a moment. Keep in mind however, that this will not work on a lead who is not completely sold, and that still requires further CONVINCING, not JUST encouragement.




Caveat


The stronger your frame is, the more you can ask of a prospect.


If your frame is not so strong, they’re not going to, for example, give you a long emotional paragraph on why they want to buy your product.




Embedded Commands


Every single thing we hear is registered as input subconsciously, even if we are not conscious of it.


This includes language.


We can include commands in our language, “embedded” covertly in our sentences and phrases.


These commands have a powerful effect, because they are not perceived as overt commands — so the natural human resistance to orders is not activated.


They are shrouded.


But the effect on the psyche is just as powerful.


They are utilized to condition the human mind towards taking a specific action or believing a specific idea.


Once you start seeing these embedded commands in language, you cannot unsee them.


The best way to learn these is to explore a lexicon of command words and see examples of how they are utilized in sentences and phrases.




Words and Phrases That Can Be Used as Commands


They can be adjectives or verbs:


“Imagine”


“Change is easy”


“Feeling comfortable”


“Think”


“Look”


“Feel”


“Relax”


“Like me”


“Get”


“Take”


“Interesting”


“Slow down”


“Quickly buy”


“Consider this”


List is endless…




Examples of Embedded Commands in Sentences


* “You know what’s interesting? I have a…”
   * You assume that the next thing I will say is interesting.


* “You, like me, can understand…”
   * You are commanding the listener to like you.


* “Don’t think of a pink elephant”
   * You just thought of a pink elephant.


* “Would you take me to the cinema?”
   * You just got told to “take me” to the cinema.


* “If I want you to learn something easily, I’m going to need you to pay close attention”
   * You just got told to “learn something easily”.


* “Imagine the feeling when you achieve your dreams”
   * You just got told to imagine the feeling of achieving your dreams




Example in Copywriting




“When I tell my members to buy X during Y, they all quickly buy and make money.


When I tell my members to buy A during B, they all quickly buy and make money.


When I tell my members to buy C during D, they all quickly buy and make money”




Can you spot the embedded command?


“Quickly buy and make money”


This is part of some sales copy for a crypto offer VSL.


Here are 3 examples (the number 3 is important as well, see 3 Yes Rule) of coins the crypto expert tells his members to buy, and their resulting action from that recommendation.


Each line is said with a different tone of voice to keep things interesting.


But the sub-communication is that they will “quickly buy and make money”.


That idea is hammered into their subconscious.


So what do you think happens when he asks you later in the VSL to “quickly buy” his crypto group so that you can “make money”?




These Are Only Useful Insofar As They Advance Your Agenda


It doesn’t make sense to insert these embedded for fun, that is a waste.


They should be utilized effectively to insert the mind frames you want your reader/listener to accept.




Juxtapose Hopeless Scenario


A lot of the time you’re going to find people who don’t believe that a product will work for them.


For us, it may seem irrational because we’ve achieved some baseline success.


But the reality is that for 99% of the world, they have NEVER seen ANYONE around them come up and become successful in whatever it is they want to achieve.


For these people it’s important that you juxtapose someone who started in a worse position than them…




As an example…


“You know what’s interesting, I was actually speaking with someone from <YOUR LOCATION> yesterday, and he was starting with only $45, had a newborn baby on the way, and was hesitant to join for the exact same reason you’re telling me right now… do you know how we helped him out?”


“no, how?”


“basically I told him that I would assign him a 1-on-1 support agent, and guarantee that he makes his entry fee back within 14 days or his money back, would that help you get over the line?”


“well, yes…”


“and you know what, he was in a much worse position than you, and now, 3 months later he’s making more money than he ever has in his life… do you really think this is hard to do?”




A few takeaways from this…


You are doing a few things here.


1. Showing him that his dream is possible.
2. Instilling subtle shame in himself that someone that is very much LIKE HIM in an even WORSE position than him was successful.
3. Giving him 3 forms of risk reversal:
   1. The story is risk reversal in itself.
   2. 1-on-1 support agent to guarantee success.
   3. Money back guarantee.
4. Finally you are placing the burden of proof on him each time.




Caveat


This technique works in two scenarios.


1. When the person is hesitating, scared, nervous — in other words, not a real or specific objection. Then the purpose of this technique is to motivate them to join.
2. When the person has a specific objection. In this case you address the objection by telling the story of having solved it for the person who was in a worse position than the person you are telling the story to.


Another caveat is that when you need good storytelling to tell the story of the person you are juxtaposing the lead’s scenario to.


Include details, it makes the story more believable.




Closing Notes


There are 3 different classes of people.


1. People who are inspired by people doing better than them, and that motivates them. (Moving towards reward.)
2. People who are infuriated that there are people more disadvantaged than them who are doing better than them. (Moving away from pain.)
3. Usually a mix of both.


Most broke people are moving away from pain, which is why they even have these types of objections in the first place.


Which is also why most people achieve a baseline level of success, whatever that looks like for them, and then they coast.


Brilliance is so rare, it’s always the people who are moving towards something, some reward that have this mindset.


But it’s also the people who are moving away from a deep depression, or moving away from the pain of sexual frustration… that become brilliant.


Selling those is a totally different sell, and a topic of a different lesson.




How to Beat Fixed Beliefs


There are certain beliefs people hold which are fixed.


And no matter how hard you try, no matter how strong your arguments or emotional appeals…


You will not be able to beat them.


So what do you do instead?


You work WITH these beliefs.


How do you do this?


Let’s look at some examples…




Example 1:


Let’s say someone has it firmly in their mind that their friend is successful and an authority on money and wants to learn from them.


You’re not going to convince them to follow you instead, especially if they are not successful themselves they have no IRL reference of anyone else who is successful.


For them that successful friend is the bees knees.


So what you do to sell them your money making offer, is tell them that he can look impressive and useful to his friend if he brings him this opportunity.


But you should also tell him that he should gatekeep this opportunity, so that he remains useful to his friend.


Because successful people want to be around useful people, and they will trust these people with more and more things, and share with them more and more secrets, right?




Example 2:


Let’s say someone firmly believes in Islam and won’t change their mind.


Well, in fact this scenario is basically standard practice so here’s how you deal with it.


You get a basic understanding of the Quran and their religion, and mold your sell around those values… but WITHOUT explicitly stating their religion as a selling point because it can seem disingenous and manipulative (unless this is the topic of conversation already and they are ready to accept it).


Some of the values are brotherhood, integrity, and family.


But they also have firm stances against alcohol, degeneracy, gambling, and premarital intimate relationships.


So just keep that in mind.


If you’re selling a money making opportunity, make sure to carefully position it as not gambling, totally ethical, and halal… otherwise good luck selling to Muslims.




Trivialize Benefits


No matter how good something is, you can always trivialize the benefits to blow your prospects’ minds.


The prospect is coming to you thinking that your product will provide X benefit, and solve Y problem.


Assure them that X benefit is assumed, and that if they are still worrying about problem Y they are thinking SMALL.


Present them NEW ADVANCED problem Z. Elaborate on the details of problem Z.


The magic of this is that by focusing on problem Z, you ASSUME benefit X provided and Y problem solved is a FOREGONE CONCLUSION.




Example with Money


“Yea we have a 95% success rate on people making money with our Ecom methods. Our process is basically guaranteed, but we get people coming back to us always with much bigger problems such as banking and payment processing. If you had to withdraw 500k from your payment processor, and into your bank account, and be able to use that to pay for advertisements… all within 48 hours, could you do that?”




Example with Dating


“Yea the interesting thing is that you’re gonna start going on a bunch of dates and sleeping with some gorgeous women, but you’ll run into one big problem. They’re going to fall in love with you and want to spend time together. You might think this isn’t a big problem now cause you’re just starting, but in a few months this is gonna be your reality and you’re going to have to learn organizational systems for your time, and filtration systems for the kind of girls you WILL want, because you don’t even know what you want yet. You’ve never reached that point, or have you?”




Conclusion


I hardly see anyone using this method in marketing and sales.


It should always be utilized because it…


- Reinforces your expert status by showing them they are not so far along the path compared to you.
- Inspires/shames them to see a new reality that is possible.
- There are ALWAYS more problems once you solve the original problem.


And it doesn’t always have to be aggressive.


It can be done subtly as well through storytelling.




If They Can’t Explain It, It Doesn’t Exist


Most of the time people do not have valid reasons for objections.


If you ask them to explain why they believe something, and they cannot explain it… Then the objection goes away.


You have to say it with a tone of confusion, and that is only possible because of the iron conviction you have in your product. This is key.


You want them to explain to you because the feasibility of their objection doesn’t even enter your reality.


By the time they finish trying to explain their objection, they’ll realize that they can’t and they’ll forget about it or change subjects.


Objection beaten ✅




Examples:


* “I’m not sure if this product will work for me…”
   * Please explain to me how the best possible product which has produced endless wins for its members is not going to work for you.
* “Why is the price so high?
   * What do you mean the price is high? Please explain to me.
   * “Oh, it’s 7000 dollars…”
   * But, and you’re getting X, Y, and Z benefit, with the most unbeatable track record in the world proven to make thousands of people millions of dollars, I don’t understand, please explain to me how it’s too expensive.
   * “Oh but it’s a lot of money.”
   * But for the value that’s inside and the results that you get, I don’t understand how that’s a lot of money. I’m here to understand you, please explain to me how it’s expensive.
* “I need to think about it”
   * Please explain to me what you need to think about. I don’t understand.




Ask X or Y (Hypnosis Trick)


This one’s a fun trick that makes you look like a genius in front of your prospect.


Use this only deeper in a conversation, once they trust you a little bit and there is rapport built.


Best way to show this is in an example:


* “Are you the oldest sibling, or only child?”
   * “Oldest sibling/only child..”
      * “I could tell. You have a responsible vibe that only a single or oldest child would have.”
      * Now you sound like a genius for guessing this.
      * Can insert any justification for your reasoning which fits.
   * “Not the oldest sibling/only child.”
      * No loss, it only looks like you’re asking if they’re the oldest or an only child.


* “Are you into crypto or stocks?
   * “Neither”
      * “Ok, then you need to be invested in blah blah…”
   * “Crypto, why?”
      * “I could tell by how you spoke you’re into investments.”


* “You’re American?”
   * “No”
      * No loss.
   * “Yes”
      * I could tell.
      * “Why?”
      * Don’t answer him, mystery.


This is an authority and credibility builder on questions you can’t lose on.


It’s what hypnotists and psychologists do to get things “right”.


No doesn’t hurt.


Yes makes you look like you understand the lead, and gives you authority.




Caveat:


You must justify HOW you know the specific observation, at least at a superficial level every time.


UNLESS your “guess” about them is so magical and totally blows them away.


In this case you can just give them a “;)” or “ah, so you see” or another derivative of a tease answer, and then keep it moving.


If someone tells you things about yourself that you would never imagine anyone could guess, you’re not going to be pressing them on HOW they know — you’re too blown away.


But if someone tells you things about yourself that are not so difficult to guess, you’ll either expect it, or wonder how they know.




Play On Their Ego


People generally always have some sort of ego. It’s extremely rare to meet someone with no ego.


In fact, it is almost impossible. So assume people have one.


People have egos about their success with money, with women, with status, and with the things they believe in.


Rich or poor, everyone has this.




How To Play On Their Ego?


You have to first identify their belief systems and personality.


What do they value, and what are they proud of?


You can do this by fact finding (see Fact Finding).




Some Things To Look Out For:


Where are they from? Their nationality can tell you a lot about what they value.


What do they believe in? Maybe some religion, cult, political affiliation, or faith.


What do they do on a daily basis? What people DO will tell you a lot more than what they will SAY.


How old are they? Age will play a factor in amplifying egotistical traits, however it can go both ways. Older people can have deeply entrenched egos, or they could have resolved and let go of these egos.


What have they accomplished? People are always proud of their accomplishments, especially since they have likely put a lot of effort into it.


What are their aspirations and goals? These are the things that people dream about, the first things they think about in the morning, and the last things that keep them up at night.




Once You Understand Them…


You can tailor your pitch to play on their ego.


However a big warning: Your offer must be possible to appeal their ego.


If you have a way to make big money, and this is what your prospect wants as well, great.


However, if the way to make big money, also satisfies the prospect's desire to be recognized, and allows them to brag about the accomplishment of making money in this avenue…


That is a MUCH stronger pull than JUST making money.


But if your offer cannot even make them money at all, then appealing to their desire for recognition will not matter at all.




Specifics


You cannot autistically tell them, “This will make people recognize you”.


It’s got to be subtle.


“Yea it’s gonna make a lot of money of course, maybe not as much as OnlyFans at the start, but we don’t wanna sell our souls right?”


“Well no, of course not.”


“I don’t know about you, but I personally wanna have a business avenue that can print loads of cash AND that I could talk about with my grandparents and be proud of, you know what I mean?”


“Yea, I totally agree”


Now he is not ONLY going to make boatloads of cash, he can also be PROUD of it.




When To Do Hard Takeaway; Soft Takeaway; Or Do Nothing


A takeaway is a technique in sales where you disqualify the prospect from purchasing your product or service.


It taps into the psychology that people want what they cannot have.


There are hard takeaways where you completely disqualify the prospect from buying and end the interaction.


And there are soft takeaways where you subtly disqualify the prospect, but continue the interaction.


There are specific scenarios to use each type of takeaway, and scenarios where doing nothing at all is better.




Hard Takeaways


This is where you totally end the interaction with a takeaway disqualifying the prospect from buying.


For example:




“Yea looks like you’re not the right fit at all based on telling me X, wish you the best of luck.”


Or


“We only offer Lamborghini to qualified clientele. Thanks for your visit.”




Sometimes a hard takeaway is required when the prospect is being stubborn, and continuing to try and convince them to buy would lose you frame.


In that case it’s better to do a hard takeaway and end the interaction.


The goal with the hard takeaway, is to trigger the prospect so that they either:


- Beg to continue the interaction, thus giving you a stronger frame once again
- Leave, but have a splinter installed in their mind and piss them off a little bit… so they come back later as a more malleable prospect


However if they do not even want what you offer them in the first place, a hard takeaway will backfire.


It will only piss them off, and you will lose the lead.




Soft Takeaways


This is where you subtly disqualify the prospect, but continue the interaction.


This is more useful in the middle of a conversation and when the prospect is not quite convinced of your offer yet.


Since this is the case, you cannot disqualify them too hard with a hard takeaway or else you will lose the lead.


So you do a soft takeaway, like this:




“If a person is negative, they wouldn’t fit well into the group mentorship and bring everyone down, don’t you agree?”


“How soon you want to solve your problem is totally up to you.”


“If you can get into our program, you will do well”


“Well it’s quite rare people meet all the requirements, so let’s see if you meet them”




The basic structure is that you want to be indirect in telling them that you doubt they would be qualified for your product/service.


WITHOUT ending the conversation. Keep talking after you deliver the soft takeaway.




Do Nothing


Sometimes it is smarter to NOT use a takeaway because the prospect is not yet bought in to your offer past a certain threshold.


This threshold is the point that you can use a takeaway, soft, or hard, without the lead just getting pissed off and either ending the conversation on you, or losing respect for you and thus losing you frame in the conversation.


You will only learn this threshold through experience and feedback, because it is unique to each lead.




Curveballs


Curveballs are when you ask abstract questions that are not in the natural or ordinary expected sequence of questions.


Questions that don’t make sense, such as “How did you sleep last night?”


These have a few uses:


* They can serve to pattern interrupt a prospect if they are getting bored or distracted in the conversation; something to make their ears prick up and pay attention again
* If you have been losing frame in the conversation, they can serve to pattern interrupt in order to have the lead pay attention to you again, so that you can take the conversation in a different direction and re-establish frame and authority.


Whatever you use as the curveball question, now serves as a point of contention in the prospects mind as well.


Because for example when you are selling an online money making course, and ask for example “How well do you sleep at night?”


Although it’s a curveball question, and is totally unrelated to the conversation.


If they don’t close, and next time the prospect has trouble sleeping…


Who's online money making course is he going to think about?


Another course, or YOUR course?




The Power Of The 3rd Person


When you are speaking in the 3rd person you can get away with a LOT more versus telling someone something directly.


But it is not always effective. Let us learn exactly how and when to use this…




The Art Of Inconspicuousness


If someone can tell that you are obviously speaking about them in the 3rd person, they will be offended and it will damage your interaction.


You should always have a plausible deniability when using this tactic.


There are multiple factors to keep in mind in order to achieve this:


* Do not speak about the topic which you know will trigger a person too soon after hearing it, especially if they have told you this information explicitly.
* If you can make inferences about this triggering information, instead of having someone tell you directly it’s even better. Because they will know they never told you anything related to this, and will not suspect that you are targeting them with your 3rd person speak, however it will still trigger them because the information is accurate.
* If you are wondering how to make accurate inferences about someone, it comes with experience.
* In any case, even if you receive this information explicitly from the person or not, it’s always best practice to deliver the information in 3rd person without including specific details about the person who you are targeting. However, do include enough details that they can still become triggered. This is a fine balance you will learn through experience.




Example


You are speaking to a person you know is not successful in business, and this is because he is hesitant about making decisions:


“So let me tell you a bit more about our ideal candidate. This person is committed, hard working, and more importantly decisive. He doesn’t really spend too much time thinking about a decision because there is a difference between people who think, and people who do. The people who do get all the spoils, while the people who think are literally still thinking. This person also has a good understanding of working with people, in other words they are not autistic, and can understand emotions and nuance…”


Here the 3rd person is a good way to do a subtle takeaway, do you spot it?




Caveat


There are some people who you cannot speak to in 3rd person because they are very astute and will spot it immediately.


With these people be straightforward about your product, how it will benefit them, and address any concerns they may have.




Preemptive Semantics


This is when you use language to assume an outcome of a future event.


For example you can say “when you join our program…”.


You are subtly assuming that they will join your program.


This will not have such an effect on people who can recognize what you’re doing, but MOST people will be subtly influenced by this.


Small details matter, this will help them imagine being inside your program already.




More Complex Example


You can get more elaborate with this technique, and describe a scenario where the prospect is already inside your program, and using the features.


“The more money you make inside using the modules, the more modules you can unlock with further teachings which will help you solve the new problems you will have that come with scaling this business model”


Assuming he’ll be using the modules, assuming he’ll be making money, assuming he’ll unlock modules, assuming he’ll have more problems that result from success in the business avenue.




MYSTERY Sell


Mystery is more about what you don’t say, rather than what you DO say.


Whatever someone can imagine a product or service can provide them, is infinitely more powerful than whatever it ACTUALLY is.


Remember, people buy things because it makes them feel a certain way ultimately.


Because the outcome provided by the offer makes them feel an emotion.


If you can make them imagine that the product or service is better than meets the eye, this will increase your desire and make them more likely to buy.




So How Do You Sell Using Mystery?


Prospects have to first want your product or service, or you, or whatever you are selling because there are loads of mysteries in our lives, but we don’t care about most of them.


Let’s assume you have something people want.


Tell them enough about it that they are sold on it.


But leave enough hidden, that they are still wanting for more details.


Now hint at these details.


For example,


* “Yea this course will make you a lot of money, but that is only the beginning”
   * “Beginning of what?”
   * “Do you still believe in money, or are you after power?”
* “It’s too expensive”
   * “It’s pennies”
   * “To me it’s not”
   * “To us it is. You think we need money?”
   * “If you knew what you’d learn you’d understand it’s pennies and pay 100x more”
   * “It if it’s not pennies to you then you need us more than ever”
   * “You think X price is money?”
* “We offer you a full refund if you don’t make money within 7.75 days.”
   * “Why so specific?”
   * “Once you are inside you will understand.”
* “I’m not sure about this business model, I think it won’t make me as much money as Y business model.”
   * “That’s a very limited view about a level of money you have not yet experienced. Tell me about Y business avenue, can it do Z?”
* “I was thinking of joining X instead…”
   * “I recommend you join X, because once you are inside you will understand you needed us all along more than ever.”
* I could go on forever…






Logic Ladders


Logic ladders are the name I use to describe the logical sequence when you explain something to a retard.


Retards cannot connect the dots for themselves, you must connect the dots for them.


Every little step must be explained in enough granular detail for them to understand.


So for example, you cannot just tell someone “If you post Youtube videos, you can make money on Youtube with your offer”.


You must explain to them using a logic ladder:


You make Youtube videos according to a certain framework -> Viewers watch the video and the CTAs in the video compel them to click on the link in the bio -> the link in the bio send them to a landing page with a VSL which explains to them the offer -> the VSL compels them to book in a call to find out more about the offer -> they get on the call and are closed on the offer they are being sold -> they send money via crypto, bank transfer, or card -> you receive the money


And boom, you just made money with your offer posting Youtube videos.




Objection Handling (How to ACTUALLY think about this)


People rarely tell you the upfront truth about what is actually holding them back from purchasing; AKA their objections.


They’ll give you some smokescreen responses such as “I have to think about it”, or “now is not the right time”.


Usually TRUE objections are things you have not addressed already in your sales pitch, which you should have based on your fact finding stage (see Fact Finding).


A proper Fact Finding stage will preempt many objections, so when you get to the close there are almost none to deal with.


Remember that proper preparation is always better than trying to solve problems after.


If you do need to handle TRUE objections later, this is how you can address the common ones best:


* “It’s too expensive”
   * This is a problem of you not building the value of the product up enough in the prospects eyes. Curveball/BOP, and go back over the features and benefits as it relates to solving their problems. But remember, you are restrained to the limits of the market valuation as well. Words can only help so much.
   * “Really? Can you explain to me why it is so expensive?
   * “Well because X.”
   * “I understand. And you have a point about X, but have you considered…”


* “How will I know I will make my money back”
   * This one is a trust and value objection. The strongest reply is telling them that making their money back is guaranteed.
   * “You are guaranteed to make your money back within X days, or your money back.”
   * If you cannot offer a guarantee, the next best thing is to tell the prospect stories of other people who made their investment back and more. This builds trust and value.
   * “Well look at Timmy over here, he joined the program 2 weeks ago and has already made X amount in commissions. And look at Mark here…”
   * If you cannot promise them their money back for one reason or another, you must place the burden of proof on them, and do a subtle takeaway and sell the mechanism harder.
   * “Well how many hours are you willing to dedicate to this?”
   * “About X hours”
   * “I recommend you do not join if you only have X hours to put in, you require at least X hours per day to be fully dedicated to this because…”


* “I’m not the decision maker”
   * This one is your fault for not identifying the decision maker earlier.
   * But it is also true that sometimes you will not be speaking to the decision maker right off the bat, especially when you are doing larger deals which involve multiple people to be onboard. If this is the case then you must sell each person successfully the way they want to be sold, until you reach the decision maker.
   * So you acknowledge, seed a future meeting, and make sure the current person you are speaking to is sold on the product/service and doesn’t have any further objections.
   * “Alright, I understand. Let’s have a chat with him/her soon then, but as far as you understand everything I’m telling you, is everything clear? Do you have any questions?”


* “We’re happy with what we have”
   * This is because you haven’t built up the pain of their problem enough. You haven’t made them understand that their current solution isn’t adequate for their problem. And you haven’t inspired them that a better solution is possible.
   * Really, you’re doing damage control here if you get this objection.
   * How you handle it is you first ask them what’s good about their solution. This is to lower their guard so it doesn’t sound like you’re immediately attacking their solution, since if they are happy with it, there is some positive emotion it is providing them.
   * Next, you ask what’s about the product. This is the natural thing to ask after asking what’s good about it, presupposing a “pros-con” framework that is widely accepted. Also keep in mind that the last thing you are asking them now is about the bad things, which remains the most relevant thing in their minds (see Tattooed Endings).
   * Now you have ammo to sell them on why their current solution is less than perfect, creating pain of having an inadequately solved problem.
   * “Ok, interesting. What’s so good about your solution?”
   * “It’s good because X, Y, and Z.”
   * “And obviously no product is absolutely perfect, what’s bad about it?”
   * “Well it’s bad because X, well actually Y too…”


* “Need to consult with my partner/wife”
   * Similar to the “I’m not the decision maker objection”, but more emotional because this is about their significant other.
   * Here this is also a fact finding issue, because if you are getting this objection often in your target demographic, then you should screen for people’s marital status beforehand to get a grasp for their mindset.
   * You need to have some experience and intuition as to how relationship dynamics function for you to accurately handle this objection.
   * For a man:
   * If the man is in control of the relationship: First ask if other than asking their wife about this decision, if there is anything else they had hesitations or questions about. If they tell you they do, handle them. If they don’t, then ask them why they even need to ask their wives. They will reply no, of course not. And then they should close.
   * “Okay, other than asking your wife, is there anything else you had questions or hesitations about?
   * “If there are no more questions, then why do you need to ask your wife? Does she make your decisions for you?”
   * If the man is NOT in control of the relationship: First ask if other than asking their wife about this decision, if there is anything else they had hesitations or questions about. If they tell you they do, handle them. If they don’t, then ask them to call their wife right now, or to even bring her on the call together to make this decision together. Insist on this, it’s very important to have them together.
   * Once they are on a call together with you, then start speaking to the wife, introduce yourself, introduce the product and state how you agreed with the husband that it is a good fit for them, and ask the wife if she has any questions or hesitations. If she does, handle them. Otherwise, ask the wife “so, are we ready to get started?” while you look at the husband. What this does is since the husband is already sold on the product, even if the wife isn’t fully onboard, she’s not going to humiliate her husband by making the decision for him. And if she tries to, the husband will be TOO humiliated and his base masculinity will override, causing him to take control and win you the sale.


* “I’ve never heard of your company”
   * Problem in your presentation. You shouldn’t really be getting this objection if you are doing your pitch properly.
   * In any case, to handle this is easy, agree and add a sell with an intriguing analogy. That intrigue is your sell to then ask them if they want more information, they will say yes basically no matter what then you have time to educate them.
   * “Well of course, you haven’t heard of bitcoin in 2012 either right?
   * “Well no…”
   * “Want me to explain more about us?”


* “How do I know this is going to work for me?”
   * This is because they do not believe the product is going to work for them. They don’t trust you enough. Keep in mind this is not a value objection. They already assume it will work, just not necessarily for them.
   * The best way to handle this is to show them other people in similar, or worse situations than them that have had success, and then place the Burden of Proof on them to qualify themselves about being capable of getting similar results themselves.
   * “Well take a look at X… he started in a worse position than you and still achieved Y results in Z time.”
   * “Do you think that given every advantage like he had, that you would also be able to accomplish something similar or better?”


* “We’ve had bad experiences in the past with something similar.”
   * This is nothing other than having a higher than normal trust barrier built up because of previous negative experiences.
   * Keep in mind that some people’s trust barrier is so high, that almost nothing will convince them instantly. Those trust barriers can take time, so learn to play a long term game as well (see Long Term Game).
   * To handle this, first disarm them, and disassociate their previous negative experiences  from you through a Burden of Proof question. Then you can show them how your product/service is different, better, and build trust with the lead again.
   * “I understand we’ve all had negative experiences before. But let me ask you, is it fair to compare us with those experiences when our product/service is completely different?”


* “I’m not ready to make a decision yet”
   * This is not a real objection. Ask them what the real objection is.
   * “What’s holding you back?”




* “I need to think about it”
   * The pain of their problem is not high enough. If you broke your leg, and the doctor offers you surgery to fix it, how long do you think you’ll need to “think” about the decision? Not very long right?
   * There are a few ways to address this objection. The first one is by using FOMO (see Fear Of Missing Out) to increase the pain of their problem. First agree with their desire to think about it, and then add a condition in which thinking about it is actually a great negative that successful members never do.
   * “I encourage you to think, because that is the exact opposite of what every member who achieved great success in the program did, and that is exactly WHY they joined and achieved great success in the first place. So we only want more of THOSE people, NOT others.”
   * The second way to address this is to increase the pain of their problem by walking them through the potential scenario if they delay their decision. But you must first agree with their desire to think, then use a specific spell (see Spells) written in Burden of Proof format.
   * “We all need to think about things, but how many times have we taken our times to think about something, only to realize in the end that we knew the correct decision to make all along?”
   * “Lots of times.”
   * “Allow me to help your thinking, will your problem go away by delaying this decision?”
   * “No.”
   * “Are you missing any information at all to make this decision?”
   * “No.” (If yes, give them the information that they need.)
   * “Then what is holding you back from making a decision now?”


* “This is not my priority right now”
   * The pain of their problem is not high enough; this is the primary reason for this objection because if their pain is high enough then they will take almost any good enough solution. But a secondary reason is that the perceived value is not high enough as well, because SOMETHING needs to solve their problem, and they will always prioritize the solution which they perceive as the highest value.
   * You can handle this objection in a unique way. First you can throw them a curveball/BOP (that also preempts that they are interested in your product/service) to reconfirm that they have a problem. They will reiterate their problem to you, and then you can increase the pain of the problem by asking them why they haven’t solved it yet. Usually they have already made some attempt at solving it, so you will learn what it is they have tried, and now all you have to do is juxtapose your solution to what they have tried, and whatever they may potentially try, and show them that your product/service is superior.
   * “Then why are you even on this call with me and interested in our product/service?”
   * “It’s because I have X problem.”
   * “Why haven’t you solved it yet?”
   * “Well I have tried X, Y, and Z, but none of them have worked for me. I’m not sure what more I need to do.”
   * “It’s not going to fix itself right. You are in the right place and the right time because our product/service is different from everything else and is better in X, Y, and Z ways…”


* “How much free time do I need”
   * This one is simple, just tell the truth. But tell the truth also with a sell no matter what the time commitment is.
   * If there is a low time commitment, you can tell them that for something so valuable that surely they can commit at least the minimum amount of time required to ensure success.
   * If there is a high time commitment, you can tell them that this program is only for the serious and dedicated individuals who are ready to achieve the greatest success.


* “I’ll do this myself”
   * This is probably one of the biggest failures you can have as a salesman. Yet still there are replies to this, you just have a bigger hill to climb.
   * What you want to do is semi-agree with them, and then ask them what their plan to do it themselves is. And then you can insert FUD (see FUD) into their plans.
   * “I certainly invite you to try. What would your plan to accomplish this yourself be?”
   * “Well I will do X, Y, and then Z.”
   * “There’s an interesting thing about using X first, you see…”


* “Do you get commission from this?”
   * Here this is a lack of trust from the lead. They do not fully believe you have their best interests in mind. Everyone knows salesmen get commission for closing a sale, but they only verbalize it in this situation. You have to make them trust you by agreeing with them, by making them understand the that decision is still theirs, and they should have full information before they make a decision.
   * “Of course I do, but the decision is still yours. Nobody is forcing you to send money for this product/service. You buy this product/service because you WANT to right? My only job is in giving you all of the accurate information so that you can make an informed decision with full information.




* “I have money in other investments, can I wait for them to go up?”
   * Here the pain of their problem is not great enough. So you have 2 objectives to accomplish with tackling this objection.
   * First you must insert FUD into either the investment itself, or the idea of investing in the first place. This requires you to have a better grasp of the particular investment, and the opportunity cost of investing.
   * Next you must increase the pain of their problem by asking them that regardless if their investments go up, will their problem be solved? Is their investments going up more important than solving their problem? Is the time spent waiting for their investments worth it?
   * Typically people who are into investments have a risk/reward mindset, so you can also play into that in the sale.
   * “X investment has been known to underperform for years, tell me again why are you invested in it?”
   * “I am invested because of X, but I’m not sure now.”
   * “So if you are not 100% sure of this investment, how could you be sure of the next one? Or if investing at all in your current position in life is worth it. The opportunity cost of investing in this asset… does it outweigh the costs of not solving your problem for the amount of time it takes your asset to MAYBE perform how you expect it to? Again, you don’t control the markets, right?”
   * “How much return do you expect to get on your money that you’ve invested? 100%? That’s still only a 2x on your money. At that level of money it is still small and insignificant. The things you can buy remain virtually the same. Your life does not change. However, solving your problem will change your life drastically, is this not true?”
